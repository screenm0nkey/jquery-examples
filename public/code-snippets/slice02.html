Benedick's Question: I pray you, what is he?
Beatrice's Answer: Why, he is the prince's jester: a very dull fool; only his gif is in devising impossible slanders: none but libertines delight in him; and the commendation is not in his wit, but in his villany; for he both pleases men and angers them, and then they laugh at him and beat him. I am sure he is in the fleet: I would he had boarded me. (Much Ado About Nothing, II.1)
Benedick's Question: I do love nothing in the world so well as you: is not that strange?
Beatrice's Answer: As strange as the thing I know not. It were as possible for me to say I loved nothing so well as you: but believe me not; and yet I lie not; I confess nothing, nor I deny nothing. (Much Ado About Nothing, IV.1)
